## Onboarding skeleton (consistent across all 6 steps)

A single layout chassis the steps reuse:

| Zone | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Progress dashes** (top) | 6 horizontal dash segments + an "N/6" counter top-right. Completed segments fill in **sage green** (visually adjacent to `--moegi`). Current segment shows its proportional fill (animates within the segment for in-step progress). |
| **Heading** | Large near-black sans, ~36–40 pt, weight ≈ 800, generous tracking. 2–3 lines max per step. |
| **Sub-line** | Mid-gray sans body, ~17 pt, italic-free, two lines max. Explains *why* the question matters ("Body data affects metabolism estimates, so calorie guidance fits you better"). |
| **Content stage** | Center of the page; varies dramatically per step (cutout demo, Live Photo, selection cards, wheel picker, ruler picker, tap-edit card). |
| **CTA** | Bottom-fixed full-width sumi pill — "Next →" through step 5, "Finish" on step 6. White label, ~58 pt height. |
| **Back affordance** | A small **white circle with sumi chevron** sits bottom-left, ~44 pt diameter. Absent on step 1 (no prior step). |
| **Disabled CTA** | When the next step requires a selection that hasn't been made (step 3 selecting gender), the CTA fades to neutral mid-gray with reduced contrast. |
| **Background** | Universal `kinari`-adjacent off-white. Sub-warmth on demo screens (steps 1–2) via a soft pink radial glow; reading-heavy screens (3–6) stay clean off-white. |

Cadence between steps: each step's screen page-replaces (no horizontal slide). The progress dashes update in place. The content stage animates *in* with subject → chrome → text cascade (matches the Kinari framework's `--ease-tawami` motion vocabulary).

## Step 1 / 6 — AI meal cutout demo

**Heading:** "Track meals in a new way with AI names and calories."
**Sub-line:** "Snap, turn it into a sticker, and save it. No manual input."

**Center stage:** an autonomous demo loop showing the framework-relevant "Subject Lift" vocabulary applied to food photography. The loop cycles through different food subjects every ~1 s.

The animation states observed (in order, per subject):

1. **Detection state** — the subject (a photographed food item, drop-shadowed onto the page) is surrounded by an **animated dashed coral / kaki-iro outline** that traces the subject's silhouette. The outline appears piecewise, drawing around the subject as if "scanning" for its edge.
2. **Crystallization** — a thin **white die-cut border** appears around the subject, separating it from the dashed outline. The dashed outline fades slightly as the die-cut becomes solid.
3. **Numeric reveal** — a small white "chip" sticker drops in below the cutout containing a heavy italic warm-orange numeral (e.g., `78 kcal`, `1193 kcal`) — the numeral is the framework's `<NumericDisplay>` in kaki-iro, sitting on its own mini-sticker.
4. **Hold + swap** — the result holds for ~600 ms, then crossfades to the next subject and the cycle restarts.

**Background:** dot-grid canvas (your `<DotGridCanvas>`), with an additional soft **radial pink wash** centered on the subject — a celebratory warmth that's specifically tied to the AI-completion moment, not present on plainer onboarding steps.

**Pattern names this corresponds to in the framework:**
- `<DotGridCanvas>` background ✓
- `<Sticker>` (die-cut variant) ✓
- `<NumericDisplay>` in kaki-iro ✓
- The detection animation maps to a **refinement** of `<SubjectLiftDemo>` — the dashed outline phase is a state we don't currently model (we have the shimmer-dust sweep, but not the silhouette-tracing line).

## Step 2 / 6 — Live Photo "Plog" composition

**Heading:** "Turn records into Live Photos and share your Plog."
**Sub-line:** "Create polished Live Photos in one tap, no manual editing needed."

**Center stage:** an inset rounded-rectangle "phone-within-a-phone" frame showing a Live Photo composition. The inset has:

- A **`● Live` pill** at the top-left of the inner frame (sumi-on-white, with a small reticle icon)
- A small **muted-speaker icon** bottom-right of the inner frame
- A photographic background of trees / forest seen through a window (the Live Photo subtly drifts during playback)
- Six cutout food stickers composited on top: sandwich, salad bowl (with greens), drink can, pear, etc. They're arranged in a loose grid — not aligned to a strict 2×3 layout, more like hand-placed.
- **Handwritten-style labels** beneath each sticker with a casual marker / pen font: e.g., `Berry Yogurt Cup 21:13`, `French Pancakes 20:45`, `Garden Breakfast Plate 20:06`, `Butter Croissant Box 19:29`. The font is *very* different from the body sans — it reads as informal scrapbook captions.

**Animation observed:** the cutout stickers go through a multi-state animation similar to step 1:

1. **Detection state** — each sticker is wrapped in **two concentric outline rings** (a pink/magenta inner ring + a sage/yellow-green outer ring), giving a "multi-color detection" feel distinct from step 1's single dashed coral line. Possibly the visual cue for *batch* subject detection (multiple subjects at once).
2. **Resolution** — the rings fade away; the stickers become plain die-cut white-bordered cutouts.

**New observation worth promoting:** the two-color detection ring is structurally distinct from the framework's current `<SubjectLiftDemo>` shimmer. It's a "we found several things" state that doesn't have a Kinari name yet.

**The handwritten label font** is also a distinct type role — currently the framework has 4 type roles (display serif, label chunky sans, body sans, numeric). A 5th "**marker / informal**" role for scrapbook moments would be a real addition.

## Step 3 / 6 — Gender selection (binary card pair)

**Heading:** "What is your gender?"
**Sub-line:** "Body data affects metabolism estimates, so calorie guidance fits you better."

**Center stage:** **two large vertical selection cards** side-by-side ("Female" / "Male"). Each card is:

- A tall rounded-rectangle (~140 × 320 in proportions), white background, generous internal padding.
- A **colored organic-blob shape** filling one corner — pink top-left for "Female", pale blue bottom-right for "Male". The blob has soft edges (radial-gradient feel, not a hard shape).
- The label sits centered on the card. Selected card's label is sumi 700; unselected is muted gray.
- The **selected card gains a 2 px outline stroke** in the blob's hue (pink for Female).

**Interaction:** the CTA pill below stays gray-disabled until a card is tapped; on selection, the pill animates to full sumi black ("Next →" affirmative state).

**Patterns observed:**
- This is a **`<SelectionCard>` pattern** the framework doesn't have. Two columns is the canonical layout; could extend to 3 (small, medium, large goal cards) or 4 (a personality-style grid).
- The **organic-blob corner** is its own design move — a soft personality marker that hints at the option's emotional flavor without being literal. It pairs with the framework's "imperfections are signatures" principle (P#10).

## Step 4 / 6 — Birth year wheel picker

**Heading:** "What year were you born?"
**Sub-line:** "Age helps estimate energy expenditure and health guidance."

**Center stage:** a **two-column composition** —

**Left column (derived value):**
- Small lowercase label `Age` (Georgia serif, 14 pt, muted)
- A **large heavy serif numeral** displaying the derived age — `20`, then `23` as the picker spins. Sumi black, ~80 pt, serif weight 700+. *Not* italic, *not* kaki-iro — this isn't a celebration, it's a derived fact.
- A small lowercase `yrs` below in muted gray.

**Right column (the picker):**
- A **vertical wheel scroller** — a soft gray rounded-rectangle backdrop with 5 visible years stacked vertically (e.g., 2008 ghosted at top → 2007 fainter → **2006** in focus center → 2005 fainter → 2004 ghosted at bottom).
- The **focus row** is highlighted by a **soft pill highlight** (a horizontal rounded-rectangle in a slightly different shade) with the focused year in heavier weight.
- Adjacent years fade with distance from center (opacity gradient). Edge years are nearly translucent.

**Motion observation:** the picker scrolls smoothly; the "derived age" on the left **updates live** as you scroll, not just at settle.

**Patterns observed:**
- A **`<WheelPicker>` pattern** is missing from the framework. Native iOS has one; ours doesn't.
- More interesting structurally: the **"derived-value display"** pairing — input on one side, computed dependent on the other, with live coupling. Could be a `<DerivedValue>` primitive that listens to a paired input.

## Step 5 / 6 — Height ruler picker (vertical scale)

**Heading:** "What is your height?"
**Sub-line:** "Height affects BMI, target weight range, and daily calorie suggestions."

**Center stage:** a **three-zone composition**:

**Left (derived numeric):**
- Heavy sans-serif numeral, ~80 pt sumi black: `160`, `169` (mid-scroll), `177` (settled).
- Small muted "Height cm" stacked beside in much smaller weight.
- **During fast scroll** the numeral renders with **vertical motion blur** — the digits show as ghosted/duplicated vertically, then crisp at settle. **This is a deliberate motion-blur effect on a text node** — a strong visual cue that the value is changing rapidly.

**Center (illustration):**
- A back-view illustrated figure (long-haired, in a pink top), one arm raised with palm flat above the head, representing the user marking their own height.
- The illustration **scales up proportionally with the height value** — at 160 the figure is smaller, at 177 it's notably larger. Continuous scaling, not stepped.

**Right (ruler):**
- A tall white rounded-rectangle "ruler" with tick marks down its length. Major ticks every 10 cm labeled in numbers (`150`, `160`, `170`, `180`, `190`), minor ticks every 1 cm.
- A **sage-green horizontal marker line** sits at the current value, extending beyond the ruler's right edge as a small bracket — it animates smoothly along the ruler as you scroll.

**Patterns observed:**
- A **`<RulerPicker>` pattern** — visually richer than the wheel picker. Continuous-value version.
- The **motion-blurred numeric during scroll** is a distinct effect worth its own primitive — could be a `<BlurredNumeral>` or a prop on `<NumericDisplay>` like `motionBlur={isScrolling}`.
- The **paired illustration that scales with input** is the same "derived value" pattern as step 4, but visual instead of numeric. Worth abstracting as a `<DerivedVisual scale={value/100}>` or similar.

## Step 6 / 6 — Weight entry (tap-to-edit card)

**Heading:** "Set Current Weight"
**Sub-line:** "Save your starting point so future weight, calorie, and progress analysis has context."

**Center stage:** a tall rounded-rectangle card with **three vertically stacked zones**:

1. **Iconography zone** (top): two large muted **footprint silhouettes** (left and right foot), ghosted in light gray. Subtle, almost a watermark.
2. **Prompt** (middle): a small bold sans line "Tap to enter your current weight".
3. **Input display** (lower middle): a white rounded-rectangle input field with a **thin sage-green outline** (the "valid / pre-filled" state). Inside: large heavy sans numeral `60` + small lowercase `kg`. The number is sumi black at this stage, not kaki-iro (it's an input, not a celebration).
4. **Footnote** (bottom): muted small text — "The default is your starting weight. Later weight logs will take priority on Home."

**CTA changes:** the bottom pill now says `Finish` (instead of `Next →`). The back button persists bottom-left.

**Patterns observed:**
- The **muted iconographic watermark** (the two footprints) is a soft personality move — a domain hint without taking visual weight. Could be a `<WatermarkIcon>` or a CSS treatment for `<Sticker>` with an `opacity-decoration` slot.
- The **green-outlined input** is a "validated / acceptable" state. The framework has the warm signal kaki-iro for celebrations; this introduces a *sage* signal for "ok, ready" — a different semantic from kaki-iro and worth naming.

## Cross-cutting design vocabulary surfaced by this video

### 1. Sage / moegi as the "ready / progress" signal

The progress-dash filled segments, the height-ruler marker line, the weight-input outline, and the various "validated" cues are all in the same sage-green family — visually adjacent to `colors.accents.moegi` (`#7DAE5C`). The app appears to use this as its **secondary signal color**, distinct from kaki-iro warmth.

The framework currently treats accents as one-per-project; this video suggests a real-app may use **two signal colors with distinct semantic roles**:
- **Kaki-iro warm signal:** celebration, completion, totals.
- **Sage progress signal:** in-progress, validated, ready.

This is a meaningful design decision worth either codifying (a two-signal model) or explicitly declining (the framework keeps one accent per project, sage is a "secondary state color" in this particular app's palette).

### 2. The dashed coral / multi-ring detection vocabulary

The framework's `<SubjectLiftDemo>` currently uses a **shimmer-dust sweep** as the AI-processing state. The KiteSprint app uses a **silhouette-tracing dashed outline** (step 1) or **concentric multi-color rings** (step 2) — both more legible than the shimmer for "we're finding the subject's edge." These are alternate vocabularies for the same moment.

Possible framework additions:
- `<SubjectLiftDemo scenario="silhouette-trace">` — the dashed-outline scanning state
- `<SubjectLiftDemo scenario="multi-detect">` — the concentric two-color rings for batch detection

### 3. Derived value coupling (numeric or visual)

Steps 4 and 5 both pair a user input (picker) with a **derived value display** that updates live. The framework doesn't model this — it's a deeper interaction pattern than the current `<NumericDisplay>` covers.

A new `<DerivedValue>` primitive could:
- Take a `from={inputRef}` (or directly a value prop) and a `compute={(v) => …}` transform
- Render the result in the framework's numeric or display type role
- Animate value changes with the framework's motion vocabulary

### 4. The "informal marker / scrapbook" type role

Step 2's handwritten labels (`Berry Yogurt Cup 21:13`) introduce a **5th type role** the framework currently doesn't model. The 4 existing roles are display serif, label chunky sans, body sans, numeric display. A 5th — call it `marker` or `scrapbook` — would cover hand-written-looking captions, casual annotations, "drawn-on" labels.

Type role: cursive / marker handwriting style (e.g., Caveat, Patrick Hand, custom marker font). Used sparingly for sticker captions and informal time-stamps.

### 5. Selection cards with organic blobs (`<SelectionCard>`)

Step 3's "Female / Male" cards are a clean abstraction of a binary or low-cardinality choice that doesn't fit the existing pattern library. The framework has `<QuietTabs>` and `<ThreeCircles>` but no "pick a single option from 2–4 visually-distinguished cards" pattern.

A `<SelectionCard>` pattern with:
- A `corner-blob` prop accepting an accent + corner ("top-left" / "top-right" / "bottom-left" / "bottom-right")
- A `selected` state with accent-colored stroke
- Composes naturally into a `<SelectionCardRow>` (2–4 cards across)

### 6. Picker primitives (wheel + ruler)

The wheel picker (step 4) and ruler picker (step 5) are both **value-selection primitives** the framework currently lacks. They could be:
- `<WheelPicker values={…} value={…} onChange={…}>` — vertical scrollable list with focus-center pill
- `<RulerPicker min={…} max={…} step={1} unit="cm">` — horizontal or vertical scale with marker line

Both are real-app necessities that any "personal software" project will reach for. Worth considering for v0.3.

### 7. Motion-blurred display numerals

The height picker's mid-scroll motion blur on the numeric display is a distinct primitive. Could be a `motionBlur` prop on `<NumericDisplay>` or a wrapping primitive `<BlurredOnChange>` that detects value transitions and applies a temporary blur filter to its child.

### 8. Progress-dash component (`<ProgressDashes>`)

The top-of-screen 6-segment progress bar with the "N/6" counter is a clean pattern the framework doesn't model. Used in any stepped flow (onboarding, multi-step capture, multi-step form). A `<ProgressDashes total={6} current={2}>` would slot in cleanly.

## Implications for the framework

This capture suggests **eight candidate additions / refinements** for a v0.3:

| # | Item | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `<ProgressDashes>` primitive | New | Stepped-flow progress bar |
| 2 | `<SelectionCard>` + `<SelectionCardRow>` patterns | New | Low-cardinality option picker with organic-blob corner |
| 3 | `<WheelPicker>` pattern | New | Vertical scrollable value picker (year, time, list) |
| 4 | `<RulerPicker>` pattern | New | Continuous-scale value picker (height, weight, slider) |
| 5 | `<DerivedValue>` primitive | New | Input-coupled live-updating display |
| 6 | `<NumericDisplay motionBlur>` prop | Refinement | Or a `<BlurredOnChange>` wrapper primitive |
| 7 | `<SubjectLiftDemo>` scenarios | Refinement | Add `silhouette-trace` and `multi-detect` modes |
| 8 | Type role: `marker` / `scrapbook` | Refinement | 5th type role for handwriting-style labels |

Two **cross-cutting design questions** the video opens up:
- Should the framework codify a **two-signal color model** (kaki-iro for warm signal + sage for progress signal)? Or keep one accent per project and treat sage-as-progress as that project's choice?
- Should the framework grow a `<ValidatedInput>` family — the sage-outline weight input is one example; there are likely others (the year-picker focus pill is arguably another).
