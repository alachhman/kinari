## Distilled design DNA

- **Subject-masked photographic sticker** is the unit of vocabulary. Each word is inseparable from the photo of the actual object that produced it — every entry is autobiographical.
- **Dot-grid off-white background** — same "scrapbook page" motif as KiteSprint.
- **Big serif dates as section headers** (`Feb 23`, `5월 07`, `Apr 20`) — feels like a real journal, not an app. The serif treatment contrasts with chunky sans-serif sticker labels.
- **Multicolor pastel shutter ring** is the singular branded element — like Instagram's gradient camera, but pastier and friendlier. It signals "kid-safe / playful / not corporate."
- **Polaroid + die-cut sticker as two interchangeable photo treatments** — most subjects become subject-masked cutouts, but full landscapes (Forest) fall back to a white-bordered polaroid frame. The system gracefully accommodates "untaggable" subjects.
- **Soft purple ✓ as the confirmation color.** Restrained, unusual — separates this app from the sea of system-green checkmarks.
- **Iconography:** every control is a friendly circle. Retry, confirm, discard, crop — all gray or white circles with thin-line glyphs. Touch targets are huge.
- **Multilingual respect.** Bilingual sticker captions (`Cup / 컵`) form a single typographic unit; the date headers fully localize (`Feb 23` ↔ `5월 07`). Pronunciation speaker icon is built into the sticker.
- **"Different from expected? Tap to adjust"** — a beautiful failure-mode UI. Acknowledges that AI vision is imperfect, gives the user agency, doesn't shame the model.
- **Counter copy is plain and human:** `1 Words` (yes, even when grammatically wrong) — clearly hand-coded, not localized through a CMS. Part of the charm.
- **The capture moment is ceremonial:** thin white corner brackets, soft yellow glow when the sticker appears, no skeuomorphic shutter sound implied. Quiet ritual.
